Tips & Best Practices

Timing Is Everything

Send follow-up emails within 24 hours of the meeting while details are still fresh. For critical meetings, aim to send them within a few hours.

Clear Action Items

Always include specific owners, deliverables, and due dates for action items. Use a consistent format that makes responsibilities unmistakable.

Scannable Format

Use headings, bullet points, and white space to make your email easy to scan. Bold key information like deadlines or meeting times for visibility.

Capture Decisions

Document key decisions made during the meeting to provide a reference point and prevent misunderstandings or conflicting recollections later.

Include All Participants

Send your follow-up to everyone who attended the meeting, plus relevant stakeholders who couldn't attend but need to stay informed.

Next Meeting Details

If applicable, include the date, time, and agenda for the next meeting. This helps maintain momentum and gives participants time to prepare.

Writing Tips for Professional Emails

  • Keep it concise

    Focus on key information and avoid lengthy paragraphs. Aim for a follow-up email that can be read in under 2 minutes.

  • Use a clear subject line

    Include the meeting name/topic and date in the subject line for easy reference and searchability.

  • End with a call to action

    Close with a clear next step, whether it's confirming receipt, completing action items, or preparing for the next meeting.
